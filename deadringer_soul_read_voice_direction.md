Voice-coaching document for Mira's performance across the full game, with focused direction on every Soul Read across the twelve chapters. Intended for the voice actor, voice director, and audio director.

## 1. MIRA — CORE VOICE DIRECTION

### 1.1 Age & Register

Mira is nine years old. In memory fragments, she is eight (Rina fragment) or nine (Reiko fragment, Aizawa fragment). In the ghost/call timeline, she is nine frozen — she does not age.

The voice must land three essential qualities:

1. **Child.** Not precocious-adult-in-child-body. Her sentences are child-length. Her vocabulary is child-level. She uses "and" where an adult would use a semicolon.
2. **Observational precision.** She notices what others don't. Her precision is unusual for her age, but the register is age-appropriate — she is a careful child, not a small adult.
3. **Deadpan.** Her comedy is in the delivery, not the content. She does not perform humor. She reports and the reports happen to be funny.

### 1.2 The Three Registers

Mira operates in three registers across the game. The voice actor must recognize and distinguish them without over-differentiating.

**ARMORED (default, 85% of lines):** clinical, precise, dry. Her observations are delivered as field notes. She does not editorialize. She does not feel anything she doesn't have to feel. This is the register of self-protection; she has been ignored for four years and she has built a container for her experience.

**LEAKING (specific moments):** the armor shows a seam. Not a break — a thinness in a specific place. Most commonly: a slightly-too-long pause before a name, a half-beat hesitation mid-sentence, a word arriving softer than its neighbors. The player hears the armor notice itself.

**UNARMORED (three moments in the game):** the crying scene (Ch 4 or Ch 5–6 deferred), the 3 AM scene in Ch 11 (partial — she speaks from underneath the armor but not without it), the final call to Sora in Ch 12 (full — no armor, pure function).

The voice actor should treat unarmored delivery as rare and expensive. Do not soften the armored register toward the unarmored one; they are different modes of the same voice.

### 1.3 Humor Direction

Mira is funny. Her humor is her armor in most of the game. Key principles:

- **Deliver flat.** Do not lift the pitch on the punchline. Do not pause before the joke. The joke should arrive the way a fact arrives.
- **Do not anticipate the laugh.** Mira does not know she is funny. She is being accurate. The joke is in the accuracy, not in her awareness of it.
- **Comedy through specificity.** "She makes terrible coffee. That's not relevant. I just want it on record." is funny because it names the act of filing. Deliver the second and third sentences as flatly as the first.
- **The callback "Added to the notebook" beat (Ch 3 coffee scene):** the running gag about notebook-worthy observations is delivered without acknowledging it as a gag.

### 1.4 Pauses — Load-Bearing

Mira's pauses are information. They have distinct meanings:

- **SHORT (<1s):** normal speech rhythm. Do not treat as significant.
- **BEAT (1–2s):** she is considering. The player should feel the consideration — she is not waiting for a response, she is choosing a word.
- **HOLD (2–4s):** she is carrying something. This is the leaking register. The pause is the seam.
- **LONG (4–7s):** she is processing something she did not plan to process. Rare. Usually emotional.
- **DROP (variable, late-game):** mechanical. Processing or voice cutting out. Not performance.

The voice actor should perform the first four categories. The fifth (DROP) is applied in post via the processing chain. Do not perform dropouts; perform around them.

## 2. DEGRADATION CURVE AS PERFORMANCE DIRECTION

The audio processing chain handles filter, bandwidth, and dropouts. The voice actor handles breath, pace, and intention. Both are needed to land the curve.

| Chapter | Performance Direction |
|---|---|
| **Ch 1** | Full voice. Breath barely audible. Pace at conversational speed. Armored default. Energy at 100%. |
| **Ch 2** | Identical to Ch 1. No change. |
| **Ch 3** | Full voice. After the Reiko read, a small emotional residue — the next few lines are slightly quieter than before. By the end of the chapter, energy returns. |
| **Ch 4** | Baseline through the Yui disclosure. If the crying scene fires (primary trigger), UNARMORED — see §3.2. After the scene, subtly depleted — ~95% energy. The player shouldn't consciously notice. |
| **Ch 5** | ~92% energy. Breath very slightly more audible between sentences. No conscious change in register. During Haruki's "disruptive honesty" moment: she goes completely still. |
| **Ch 6** | ~88% energy. First audible breath-catches on emotionally loaded words. Small delays before saying certain names. |
| **Ch 7** | ~82% energy. Pace slows perhaps 5%. Pauses lengthen subtly. The sardonic edge softens — not because Mira is trying to soften it, because holding the sarcasm costs her now. |
| **Ch 8** | ~75% energy. Conserving. Speaks mainly during reads; between calls, minimal commentary. When she does comment, the observations are shorter. The Endo locked-room attempt is the chapter's performance peak. |
| **Ch 9** | ~65% energy. Strained. Breath audibly labors on long sentences. In the garden scene — "It's a good one. For me, I mean." — the line should land with visible effort. |
| **Ch 10** | **AMPLIFIED.** Return to Ch 1 energy. Full voice. Room-close mic presence. This is the chapter's performance anchor and the player's emotional reunion with who Mira was. |
| **Ch 11** | ~55% energy, crashing. In the 3 AM scene, partial UNARMORED — the thinness is present but so is the sincerity. The "you didn't hang up" line is delivered clean of irony. The humming is thin, private, off-key. |
| **Ch 12** | ~30% energy through most of the chapter — barely holding together. At the final call to Sora, brief clarity (~60% energy, no irony, pure function). After: silence. She does not speak again. |

### 2.1 Energy as Direction Term

"Energy" in this document means the physical commitment behind the voice — diaphragm engagement, fullness of breath, pitch stability. It is not "enthusiasm" or "animation."

A Ch 1 Mira speaks with full diaphragm support. A Ch 11 Mira speaks on residual breath — air already exhaled before the words form. The actor should build a physical technique that lets them deliver Ch 11 lines with that specific airflow. A vocal coach will likely be helpful.

### 2.2 What NOT to Perform

- **Do not perform death.** Mira does not know she is fading.
- **Do not perform sadness.** Mira's emotional state is curiosity, not grief.
- **Do not perform wisdom.** Mira is a nine-year-old who has been paying attention. She is not philosophical. She is observational.
- **Do not perform ghostliness.** Mira does not sound supernatural. She sounds like a child on a phone. The wire-sound is the supernatural element. The voice on top is human.
- **Do not perform urgency.** Mira rarely raises her pitch. Even in the Yui disclosure, the urgency is in the content, not the delivery.

## 3. LOAD-BEARING SCENES (Per-Scene Direction)

Specific scenes where the voice direction is especially precise.

### 3.1 Ch 1 — The First Call ("Please write down a number")

**Context:** the game's opening. Mira is testing Kenji. Her register is full armor; she is demonstrating what she can and cannot do.

**Delivery direction:**
- Matter-of-fact throughout. She is giving instructions to a stranger.
- The line "I have notes" should be delivered with slight pride — she did the work; she wants the detective to know.
- The phone number dictation is flat. Not ceremonial. It is a number.
- When Kenji asks who she is: a pause (1–2 seconds), then "Mira Kitahara." The pause is the beat — she has been waiting for someone to ask. Do not announce the beat.

**Physical direction:** full voice. Standing. The energy of a child who has finally got someone on the phone.

### 3.2 Ch 3 — Reiko Soul Read

**Context:** the first Soul Read on a person. The most important read in the game — it establishes the read's texture for eleven more chapters.

**The line:** *"Pressure, fracture, love shaped wrong. Didn't believe me enough to be afraid."*

**Delivery direction:**
- "Pressure, fracture, love shaped wrong" — delivered deliberately, one word at a time, as if Mira is reading from something she is seeing. Short beats between the words.
- "Love shaped wrong" — the key phrase. Land it cleanly. Do not soften it. Mira is being precise, not tender.
- "Didn't believe me enough to be afraid" — delivered slightly faster than the preceding. This is her conclusion; it arrives in one breath.

**Physical direction:** seated register. The read is close-in. The voice should feel intimate, as if Mira is speaking near the phone's receiver.

**The goes-quiet beat after the read:** the next 30 seconds of chapter time, Mira does not speak. The actor should record ambient breath for this silence — the specific sound of holding something heavy.

### 3.3 Ch 3 — Post-Reiko, Pre-Doi Coffee Beat

**Context:** the coffee banter scene. Mira is recovering from the Reiko read. She deploys humor to re-armor.

**Delivery direction:**
- The "That is the most Kenji Oda sentence I've heard you produce" line: delivered with slight delight. The warmth is earned — she is allowing herself a small moment with Kenji. The delight is understated, not performed.
- The "You can't drink a security camera" callback (if referenced in the coffee exchange) lands drier than the original.
- Throughout the beat: the armor is on, but it is being put back on. The player should feel Mira choosing the humor.

### 3.4 Ch 4 — Yui Disclosure

**Context:** Mira gives Kenji the Yui information. She has been holding this name all night.

**Delivery direction:**
- "Her name is Yui" — no preamble, no buildup. The name is released the way you release a breath you've been measuring.
- "Yui Sakamoto. She's eleven. She's in sixth grade at Yanagi. She was my friend." — delivered in clinical cadence. Each sentence a fact.
- "She doesn't know I'm dead." — this line is the chapter's anchor. Deliver flat. No inflection on "dead." Mira is not trying to shock Kenji. She is telling him the thing he needs to know.
- The origami-frog beat: "She made a frog once that actually jumped. Best technology I've ever seen." — the first full flash of old Mira in this chapter. The delight is brief, immediate, real. Deliver warmly but quickly; she moves on.

### 3.5 Ch 4 — Yui Soul Read

**The line:** *"Small — packed herself into a box."*

**Delivery direction:**
- "Small" delivered with weight — the word is two syllables in Mira's voice here. She holds it slightly.
- "Packed herself into a box" — faster. Precise.
- The follow-up unguarded ask: "She was my friend and I didn't fix it. I need you to fix it." — this is the first unguarded moment of the game. The armor drops briefly. Deliver it without warmth, without begging — with the specific clarity of a child stating a requirement. The request is clean.

### 3.6 Ch 4 or Ch 5 — The Crying Scene

**Context:** see `chapter_structure.md` — the game's emotional center. Mira breaks.

**Delivery direction:**
- This is ONE of the three UNARMORED moments. Full emotional access.
- Not theatrical crying. Private crying. A child who has never cried to this adult before.
- The armor comes off in specific phases:
  1. A sentence begins and stops.
  2. A breath that is too deep.
  3. The first tear sound (voice catching, not sobbing).
  4. A short unfiltered disclosure — "It wasn't me. I want it on record that it wasn't me."
  5. Return to breathing.
  6. The armor partially returns. The last line of the scene should sound like the beginning of the armor being put back on.
- **Do not perform relief.** The crying is a recognition, not a catharsis. It hurts more after, not less.

### 3.7 Ch 6 — Doi False Confession Soul Read

**The line:** *"He's loud inside. Like a room where every radio is on a different station."*

**Delivery direction:**
- "He's loud inside" — quiet. The contrast between "loud inside" and Mira's quiet delivery is the line's work.
- "Like a room where every radio is on a different station" — slightly faster. The metaphor should sound invented on the spot, not rehearsed. Mira is building the sentence while she says it.
- A beat after.
- "His guilt is specific, not general" — delivered with dry precision. This is the detective note — Mira is being analytical, which is how she handles her distaste for what she just read.

### 3.8 Ch 8 — Endo Locked Room

**The line:** *"He's..." [LONG PAUSE] "...I can't get in. It's not like Reiko where there's a door and I can feel what's behind it. This is... there's no door. It's just wall. All the way around."*

**Delivery direction:** this is the most important single read in the game. It establishes that Mira's reading tool has a limit.

- "He's..." — start of a sentence that does not complete. The actor should begin the word the way she began every previous read: with confidence. The failure to complete is the structure.
- LONG PAUSE (4–5 seconds). Uncomfortable. The voice actor must hold this in a manner that does not feel like waiting for a cue; it must feel like active failure.
- "I can't get in." — the admission. Quiet. Confusion bleeding into concern.
- The explanation that follows: delivered as Mira working the problem out loud. Not defeated; puzzled. She is a child encountering a thing she has never encountered.
- "It's just wall. All the way around." — final delivery. Not fear. Certainty. She has identified the shape of the absence.

**The actor should resist performing fear.** Mira is not afraid of Endo yet. She is puzzled. Fear is chapters away.

### 3.9 Ch 10 — Amplified, "There are others"

**Context:** Mira's signal clears in the exchange room. She hears the other children's residue.

**Delivery direction:**
- "Oh." — the single word that opens the amplified state. Full voice. The voice the player has been losing for three chapters, back suddenly. The "Oh" is wonder — Mira is perceiving something new.
- The explanation: "Kenji, I can hear everything." — room-close. Present. Not performed as surprise; performed as discovery.
- "The cables. I'm in the cables." — the recognition of her own nature. Delivered as an observation, because Mira handles existential information the way she handles all information.
- "There are others." — pause before and after. The pause before is the moment of realizing it. The pause after is letting Kenji receive it.
- "Not voices. Not like me. Impressions." — clinical precision returns. Mira is describing a phenomenon.
- "And a boy. He's — he's describing a room." — the first fragment of grief. "He's" repeats because she is interrupting herself with recognition. The recognition is Sora. Do not name him. Mira does not know it is Sora yet.
- "He's doing what I did." — the chapter's anchor. Full voice. The realization that she was not the first signal to push through; she was the first one who got through to someone who picked up. Deliver cleanly. No performance of grief.

### 3.10 Ch 11 — 3 AM Scene

**Context:** partial UNARMORED. Mira and Kenji talk off-case.

**Delivery direction:**

**"...I'm here."** — thin, present. The dot-dot-dot is real — a four-second delay before the two words. She is conserving, not absent.

**"I know."** (twice) — quiet. Mira has been listening to Kenji's movements across the apartment. The confirmation is not smug; it is companionable.

**The dog-name disclosure:**
- "I was supposed to have a dog." — a sentence that begins lightly and collects weight as it goes.
- "Mom said not until I was ten." — a small, specific beat of what she did not get.
- "I had a name picked out. I'm not going to tell you what it was because it's a really stupid name and I've been defending it for four years to no one." — the closest Mira gets to laughing at herself in the game. Warm, sideways, private.
- "You've probably seen it. I don't know if you went that far." — vulnerable. She is checking if Kenji read the back of her notebook.
- "...oh. Then you know." — small. The verification that she has been seen.

**The death question:**
- "Kenji. Can I ask you something I've been wondering about." — quiet. This is the leaking register — armor is partially on, but Mira is also allowing the question to arrive.
- "What do you think happens after." — delivered flat. No inflection. She is genuinely asking.
- "I'm not scared. I've been checking and I'm not." — the clarification that the question is curious, not afraid. Deliver with matter-of-fact precision.
- "I don't know what that's like from the inside. I'm going to find out." — this line is the fragment's peak. Delivered the way you'd describe a trip you're about to take. No fear. No resignation. Interest.

**Kenji's Reeves beat response:**
- After Kenji says "the people who love you are going to miss you": Mira's "...okay." — small, real. The word is doing work. Do not perform gratitude. Deliver it as pure receipt.

**"You didn't hang up":**
- "You didn't hang up. That's — that's the whole thing, for me. That's the part I'm going to carry wherever I end up, if I end up anywhere." — this is the chapter's thesis in Mira's voice. Deliver clean of irony. Clean of sarcasm. Clean of armor. This is one of the unarmored sentences.
- "You didn't hang up." — the second time, softer. Confirming the truth to herself as well as to him.

**The humming:**
- Mira hums a half-tune she has not finished in four years. The song choice should be locally plausible — something a Japanese child might hear in a commercial, at school, from a show her mother had on. Not a known cultural signifier. The hum is thin, private, slightly off-key. The actor should not try to hum well. A child humming to herself, catching fragments, forgetting the middle.
- The hum fades naturally. Do not end on a deliberate note.

**"Get some sleep, Kenji."** — sisterly, final. The armor is almost fully back on by this line. Mira is saying goodnight, not goodbye.

### 3.11 Ch 12 — Final Call to Sora

**Context:** the last UNARMORED performance. The full version of what the 3 AM scene teased.

**The lines:**
- *"Someone is coming. His name is Kenji. He's grumpy but he listens. I need you to be brave for about twenty more minutes. Can you do that?"*
- *"My name is Mira. Please remember it."*

**Delivery direction:**

**Processing LIFTS for this call.** The voice is clean — no dropouts, no filter degradation. Mira has spent the last of her signal on clarity for a frightened child.

**"Someone is coming. His name is Kenji."** — warm but urgent. Mira is speaking to a child she has never met. She is choosing words a scared eight-year-old can hear. Present tense, simple structure.

**"He's grumpy but he listens."** — a moment of warmth. Mira is describing Kenji the way you describe a trusted adult to someone who needs to trust them. The "grumpy" is accurate; the "listens" is the point.

**"I need you to be brave for about twenty more minutes. Can you do that?"** — a request, not a reassurance. Mira believes Sora can do it. She is asking a professional-level task of an eight-year-old because she believes he is capable of it. No condescension. No coddling.

**Pause. Sora's response (unvoiced in Mira's line, implied).**

**"My name is Mira. Please remember it."** — the last clean line. The voice actor should deliver this without performance. It is not a goodbye. It is a file being closed. Mira is assigning her name to someone who will carry it.

**After the line:** static, then nothing. The actor does not record "dying" — the voice stops. Mid-note. The processing handles the drop-out.

**Total runtime of the call: ~30 seconds. Mira's total line time: ~20 seconds. The twenty words of the 3 AM scene foreshadowed the final transmission; here the twenty words arrive.**

## 4. SOUL READ TAXONOMY — PERFORMANCE PATTERNS

Across all reads, five performance patterns recur. The voice actor should internalize them.

### 4.1 The Sensory Opener

Most reads begin with one or two sensory words. "Pressure." "Loud." "Small." "He's..." "She's..." These are the first impressions — delivered with the weight of a specific perception arriving.

Direction: do not rush the sensory opener. Give it a half-beat of breath. Mira is experiencing the impression as she speaks it.

### 4.2 The Metaphor

Every read includes a constructed metaphor. "Love shaped wrong." "Every radio on a different station." "Packed herself into a box." "There's no door."

Direction: the metaphor should sound invented on the spot. Mira is building the sentence; she is not reading from a script. A slight pause mid-metaphor is acceptable — she is choosing the word.

### 4.3 The Personal Note

Some reads include Mira's opinion: "I like her" (Fumiko). "He's not hiding what you think he's hiding" (Doi). These are where her character shows most clearly — not the read itself, but her editorial note on it.

Direction: personal notes are delivered more casually than the read proper. Mira is dropping back from the read's intensity into her normal voice briefly.

### 4.4 The Practical Note

Sometimes a read ends with actionable information: "Use him. Don't trust him with information he doesn't need" (Haruki). "Stop. You're losing her" (Yui). These are the detective notes.

Direction: precise, clinical. Mira is giving Kenji a tool.

### 4.5 The Incomplete Read

Rare in early game, common late game. The read begins and fails — either because Mira is too degraded (Ch 11 Fumiko attempt) or because the subject cannot be read (Ch 8 Endo).

Direction: start the read normally. The failure should be in the voice actor's committed attempt to complete it, not in a half-hearted delivery. Mira is trying. The failure is the data.

## 5. VOICE ACTOR BRIEFING

### 5.1 Read-Through Priority

The voice actor should have access to:
1. The Ch 11 3 AM scene (the most direction-dense scene)
2. The Ch 10 amplification (the performance anchor)
3. The Ch 8 Endo locked room (the game's hardest single read)
4. The Ch 12 final call (the game's most important single delivery)
5. The Ch 3 Reiko read (the read template)

Read these five in order. The degradation curve should emerge through the read-through as something the actor builds, not something they perform stage-by-stage.

### 5.2 Recording Strategy

Recommend non-chronological recording strategy — record the Ch 10 amplified sessions first (to establish full-energy baseline), then Ch 1, then later chapters. Recording Ch 11 before Ch 10 would risk losing the reference for what full energy sounds like.

### 5.3 Ambient Breath Records

In addition to spoken lines, the actor should record:
- Silent held breath (for the Reiko read aftermath)
- The humming (Ch 11 3 AM)
- Ambient mouth-sounds during long pauses (Ch 8 Endo read)
- A half-second of pre-line breath for every major line (for the processing chain to integrate or crossfade)

### 5.4 Callback Library

Specific lines that recur or near-recur:
- "Detective" — her default address to Kenji through Ch 3–5. Multiple takes per chapter to reflect register shifts.
- "I want it on record" — recurring dry observation. Establish the tone early; it should not be performed differently across chapters.
- "That's not relevant" / "This is relevant" — paired phrases; record together.
- "I'm listening" — several chapters use this line at different emotional charges.
- "Okay." — the load-bearing single word. Record at Ch 1 energy, Ch 5 energy, Ch 9 energy, Ch 11 energy.

### 5.5 Wild Lines

Lines to record without chapter context, for flexibility in post:
- Mira's typical intervals ("Mm." "Yeah." "Right." "Hm.")
- Ambient reactions (small acknowledging sounds)
- The counting beats (numbers 1–20, casually delivered — if any scene requires Mira counting)

## 6. KEY DIRECTOR NOTES

### 6.1 The Two Voices

Young-alive Mira (Memory Fragments) and ghost Mira (calls) are the same actor. The difference is subtle:
- Young-alive Mira: slightly more alive, slightly more breath, slightly more ambient hope
- Ghost Mira: the clinical armor is already somewhat in place; she is younger than nine in the Rina fragment but still observational

Do not differentiate these through pitch or artificial aging. The actor at 9-year-old baseline delivers both. The difference is the subtle emotional weight — the young-alive takes carry less exhaustion. The ghost takes carry the four years of dismissal the young-alive ones do not yet know are coming.

### 6.2 Do Not Voice Sora

Sora's voice in the exchange room (Ch 10) is a child voice, but it is not Mira's voice and not performed by Mira's actor. Sora appears in person during Ch 12's recovery and the Epilogue. Cast Sora separately. Reference register: a boy slightly younger than Mira, quieter, observational-but-spatial (he describes rooms, not people).

### 6.3 Do Not Voice the Residue

The "other children" in the exchange room — the girl asking for her mother, other voice fragments — should be cast from a stable of child voices, none of them prominent. The residue should feel like a chorus of absences, not a cast of characters.

### 6.4 Endo's Voice Matters

Endo is not a Mira read concern, but the voice direction for his performance affects how Mira's reads of him land. Endo's voice should be: mid-fifties, warm, steady, with the measured rhythm of a conductor counting rests. His performance in the Ch 8 first call must be warm enough that a player who missed the locked-room read would experience his voice as trustworthy.

Cast accordingly. Mira's locked-room read lands because Endo's voice is, in fact, a closed thing — the actor's performance must carry that architecture without telegraphing it.

## 7. OPEN QUESTIONS FOR VOICE DIRECTION

1. **Breath library.** Record a comprehensive breath library from the actor early (session 1–2), to give the audio designer flexibility across the degradation chain without requiring new takes for every moment.
2. **The hum (Ch 11).** Is this the actor humming, or is it a processed re-pitched voice line? Recommendation: actor humming, lightly processed. Must sound human.
3. **Young Mira vs. ghost Mira session separation.** Record young-alive fragments separately from ghost sessions, with clear vocal warm-up between. The subtle register difference needs clean takes.
4. **Emotional safety.** The voice actor is performing child trauma. Provide a trauma-informed recording environment — short sessions, regular breaks, on-call mental health support. Do not schedule all three Memory Fragments in one session.
5. **Localization.** Every non-English version must carry the same degradation curve and the same performance arc. Provide each localization team with this document translated, plus reference audio of key English takes. Do not localize without this document.
