# Feedback Response: Systemic Reframing Design

**Goal:** Six targeted interventions responding to external feedback on the Higashino-lens implementation — reframing Endo from mastermind to systemic embodiment, seeding early-game recontextualization payoffs, making the Mira-Sora mirror explicit, threading the "care about a place" line, protecting the climax from mechanical weight, and adding Kenji's proto-detective recognition beat.

**Constraint:** Reframe over add. Surgical changes to existing passages. No new scenes. Three techniques: reframe (alter existing framing), echo (single line in an existing scene that rhymes with something elsewhere), recognition beat (character articulates what the reader assembled unconsciously — max 2 total).

---

## Section 1: Endo — Systemic Embodiment Over Mastermind

**Problem:** Four passages center Endo's personal agency rather than the community pathology he channels. The manuscript is 80% correct — every character's failure predates Endo — but the exchange discovery, three-leads technique, exchange room scene, and garden read as singular tradecraft rather than systemic behavior.

### Change 1A — Exchange discovery reframe
**File:** `deadringer_characters.md` (~lines 700-708)
**Current:** "Endo discovered the room fifteen years ago... He tested it. Methodically... The switchboard didn't create the Archivist. It confirmed his worldview, extended his reach."
**Reframe:** Shift from "found and mastered" to "recognized what the community was already doing." Key verb changes: "discovered" → "recognized"; "extended his reach" → "made visible the reach he already had through social architecture." The switchboard is Yanagi made literal — everyone routes information selectively, Endo found the room where the wiring is visible.

### Change 1B — Three leads technique reframe
**File:** `chapter_08.md` (~lines 127-144)
**Current:** Framed as Endo's personal algorithm ("Endo always offers exactly three leads... Three is the number that feels like generosity").
**Addition:** One sentence (design note) after the technique description observing that Rina also offers three social explanations, Aizawa files reports in threes (incident, context, recommendation), Doi gives three reasons things are fine. The "three" pattern is how Yanagi's social logic naturally portions truth. Endo is conscious of it; everyone else does it instinctively.

### Change 1C — Exchange room scene addition
**File:** `chapter_10.md` (~lines 178-205)
**Current:** "Someone has cleaned the contacts... as if Endo arranged this room the way Kenji arranges an interview."
**Addition:** One line after the Kenji/interview comparison. The room is organized the way the neighborhood is organized — the same logic of maintenance, the same careful routing, the same invisible infrastructure that makes everything arrive where it's supposed to. The room doesn't reveal Endo's secret system. It reveals the town's system with the walls removed.

### Change 1D — Garden reframe
**File:** `chapter_11.md` (~lines 487-500)
**Current:** Plant purchases mapped one-to-one with child disappearances. Reads as trophy collection.
**Addition:** No change to the confrontation evidence (mechanically necessary for the board). Add a design note or Mira observation that recontextualizes: other people in Yanagi also tend things connected to loss — Reiko's shrine shelf, Doi's wiped photograph, Yui's pressed flowers. Endo's garden is the most literal version of what the entire community does: convert children into objects you maintain. He's not unique in kind. He's unique in honesty.

## Section 2: Early-Game Recontextualization Seeds (Ch 1-4)

**Problem:** The early chapters have atmospheric wrongness (curated maintenance, Yui's silence, Doi's photograph) but most moments don't have explicit recontextualization payoffs. The "private" line (Ch 2:345) works because it pays off in Ch 10. The others sit as texture.

**Fix:** Not adding new atmospheric moments — there are enough. Adding retroactive weight by planting design notes that map payoffs, and in two cases, adding a single echo line later where the early detail cracks open.

### Change 2A — Vending machine payoff
**File:** `chapter_02.md` (~lines 63-67) + `chapter_10.md` (echo line)
**Current:** Broken since April, meander soda specificity. Pure texture.
**Addition:** Design note in Ch 2 marking as recontextualization target. In Ch 10 (exchange room discovery), one line: the vending machine was broken because its power line was rerouted to the exchange. "Broken since April" becomes evidence of infrastructure theft, not neglect.

### Change 2B — Camera coverage gap payoff
**File:** `chapter_02.md` (~lines 91-107)
**Current:** Kenji notes the gap is unusual for a well-maintained district.
**Addition:** Design note only, mapping to Ch 10. The gap isn't neglect — it's design. The exchange room entrance is in the coverage gap. No new chapter text needed.

### Change 2C — Doi's photograph connection
**File:** `chapter_03.md` (~lines 206-208)
**Current:** "Wiped so many times the surface has a faint cloudiness."
**Addition:** Design note only. Doi's photograph is his version of Endo's garden — both men maintain an object connected to a child. Echoes through Section 1D (garden reframe) where the community pattern is named.

### Change 2D — Yui's apartment echo
**File:** `chapter_04.md` (~lines 99-103) + `chapter_10.md` (echo line)
**Current:** "Careful quiet," "a held breath."
**Addition:** One echo line in Ch 10 (exchange room discovery). Mira or Kenji observes that Yui's apartment felt like this room — both spaces organized around listening, both spaces where silence is the product. One sentence. Early texture becomes structural evidence.

**Problem:** The design docs are explicit ("Mira reported. Sora mapped. Both saw the world with the kind of accuracy the system cannot tolerate.") but the chapters never articulate it. Maps carry tactical weight (infrastructure evidence) but not thematic weight (another child's system for processing danger).

### Change 3A — Kenji recognition beat (Ch 10)
**File:** `chapter_10.md` (after cable route overlay comparison)
**Placement:** Immediately after Kenji compares Sora's drawings to Fumiko's cable routes.
**Addition:** One beat (2-3 lines). Kenji's internal observation: he's looking at two parallel investigations conducted by children — one in language, one in geometry — both dismissed as imagination because the system has no category for a child who sees clearly. Not dialogue — detective insight applied to people.

### Change 3B — Ch 9 design note
**File:** `chapter_09.md` (~lines 325-335)
**Addition:** Design note marking the infrastructure echo as thematic setup for Kenji's Ch 10 recognition. No prose change — "He thought we fit" and the overlay remain as-is.

**Recognition chain completed:** Mira → Sora (Ch 10:244, "He's doing what I did"), Reiko → Mira (Ch 11:161, "She was doing your job"), Kenji → Mira (epilogue, Section 6). Three characters, three lenses.

## Section 4: Threading the "Care About a Place" Line

**Problem:** The line appears once (Ch 2:437-443). Design notes call it "the foundation for understanding Endo in late game." Never echoed.

**Constraint:** No character quotes it. Threading is behavioral, not verbal — characters unconsciously demonstrating the care/ownership distinction.

### Change 4A — Doi's bench echo

**Addition:** One line where Doi describes why he maintains the bench area. His reason sounds like care but reads as ownership to an attentive player — maintaining it because "someone should" or because it's "the right thing." The player who remembers Mira's line hears the distinction.

### Change 4B — Aizawa design note
**File:** `chapter_05.md` (Aizawa's altered report)
**Addition:** Design note connecting the report alteration to the care/ownership line. No prose change — the connection is implicit. Aizawa genuinely believes she's helping. That's the point.

### Change 4C — Exchange room echo (overlaps with 1C)
**File:** `chapter_10.md` (exchange room scene)
**Addition:** One phrase within the Section 1C reframe that structurally rhymes with "it looks the same from outside." Kenji observes that the room's maintenance is indistinguishable from care until you understand what it's maintaining. The text never points at the connection.

### Change 4D — Reiko epilogue design note
**File:** `epilogue.md` (Reiko's resolution)
**Addition:** Design note only. Reiko has crossed from ownership-care to genuine care — she stopped maintaining Mira's memory and started sitting with Mira's reality. No prose change. The epilogue already does this. The note confirms the thematic payoff.

## Section 5: Protecting the Climax from Mechanical Weight

**Problem:** The climax already handles this well — emotional recontextualization over factual revelation. The risk is not in the current text but in what the Higashino-lens implementation added: more board evidence, more partition pattern cross-references, more hidden act items. The board now has more ammunition. The danger is that evidence presentation feels like the climax when the actual climax is Reiko's notebook scene and Mira's final call.

**This section is design notes and production guidance only. No prose changes.**

### Change 5A — Board confrontation hierarchy
**File:** `chapter_11.md` (confrontation scene)
**Addition:** Design note establishing explicit emotional hierarchy. The board is Act 2's climax, not the story's. The board resolves the investigation. The notebook scene resolves Mira. The final call resolves the relationship. Evidence subordinate to grief subordinate to connection.

### Change 5B — Evidence presentation pacing
**File:** `chapter_structure.md` (Higashino-lens evidence chain section)
**Addition:** Design constraint: hidden act evidence items discoverable throughout mid-game but should NOT all converge on the board simultaneously. The board should feel like assembling fragments the player already processed emotionally, not a data dump.

### Change 5C — Notebook scene protection
**File:** `chapter_11.md` (notebook reading scene, ~lines 81-194)
**Addition:** Design note marking this as the emotional climax of the entire game. Nothing after the board confrontation should exceed this scene's emotional register. The final call operates at a different register — quiet, relational, small — and should not compete.

## Section 6: Kenji's Proto-Detective Recognition Beat

**Problem:** The recognition chain is nearly complete: Mira recognizes it in Sora (Ch 10:244, "He's doing what I did"), Reiko recognizes it about Mira (Ch 11:161, "She was doing your job... No. She was doing mine."). The character bible frames it structurally ("Two professionals with a case to close"). What's missing is Kenji's own voice — his direct recognition of Mira as a professional peer.

### Change 6A — Kenji's epilogue recognition
**File:** `epilogue.md` (Kenji's closing beat, ~lines 165-172)
**Current:** Kenji holds the dead phone, checks it, "holds it the way you hold something that used to be alive."
**Addition:** One line, direct from Kenji. He recognizes that the case file he built mirrors the notebook Mira kept — same structure, same methodology. She was nine. He finished her case the way you finish a colleague's case when they can't come back to the desk. Professional respect, not sentiment.

### Change 6B — Notebook scene design note
**File:** `chapter_11.md` (~lines 149-169, near Reiko's recognition)
**Addition:** Design note: Kenji's version of this recognition comes later, in the epilogue, at a different register — professional respect rather than maternal guilt. Two characters recognizing the same truth through different lenses.
